Follow-up to the conv200 page, which was invalidated by DexYCB sentinel-labelled untracked frames 0–4 (hand GT collapsed to wrist-relative local space, hand metric meaningless). This page retrains the same architecture on tracked frames 65–69 of the same sequence, with the bridge hardened (HandNotTrackedError) and a post-load hand/obj z-centroid sanity check (|Δz| < 30 cm) that both runs passed (hand z = 0.877 m, obj z = 0.882 m).

The head of the question the A/B tests: Should we unfreeze camera_head / point_head / depth_head during DCF fine-tuning? Both runs are 200 steps, loss-weight-scene = 1.0, DCF lr 1e-3, global_blocks lr 1e-4. A keeps the scene heads frozen (default). B unfreezes them at lr 1e-5.

TL;DR — Freezing wins on this smoke. On 5 tracked frames × 200 steps, frozen scene heads reach hand 22.5 mm / obj 20.1 mm; unfreezing scene heads at lr 1e-5 reaches only hand 134.3 mm / obj 83.8 mm. 6× worse hand, 4× worse object. Confirms the smoke-stage freezing policy added to docs/PROJECT_PLAN.md. Whether unfreezing helps on full-dataset training remains an open question (expected to flip in favour of B only once the DexYCB domain shift adds up across many frames).

Update 2026-04-15: the v1 conclusion that "freezing wins by 6×/4×" was partially artifactual. An audit revealed the scene loss here used only depth_loss + camera_loss, missing the canonical point_loss (~40% of STream3R's scene-loss signal) and ran with S=1 views (making camera_loss degenerate). The v2 A/B rerun with all 5 fixes shows the gap shrinks to 1.7×/1.2× — unfreezing is no longer destructive.

Interpretation & caveats

Smoke-stage conclusion: freeze wins by a large margin (6× hand / 4× obj). This matches the freezing policy recorded in docs/PROJECT_PLAN.md — overfitting on 5 frames with 591 M trainable params is strictly worse than 310 M. Freezing still lets scene loss reshape global_blocks via gradient through the frozen heads; the feature backbone adapts without the heads drifting.
Caveat — this does NOT generalise to full-dataset training. The reason B loses on 5 frames is the same reason it may win at full scale: the extra 281 M scene-head params give more capacity to absorb DexYCB domain shift (static camera, 518×518 non-isotropic intrinsics fx=499, fy=665). At 5 frames this capacity is pure overfit fuel; at 50 k frames it's a real adaptation lever. Phase 3 should re-run this A/B on the full sequence with an EMA-to-frozen regulariser on the head weights.
Caveat — the scene-loss scalar remains ambiguous. Both runs report negative scene-loss values due to the −α·log(conf) entropy bonus in conf_loss. For B, this is particularly dangerous since depth_conf is trainable — the optimiser could game the entropy term instead of fitting depth. Before Phase 3, split the logged scene loss into loss_conf, loss_grad, loss_camera so we can separate regression progress from entropy reshaping.
