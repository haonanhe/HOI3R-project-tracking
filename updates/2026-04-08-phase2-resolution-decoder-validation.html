Key finding: Exp B's pre-optimization hand chamfer (0.0054) is already 1.8x better than Exp A's post-optimization (0.0096). Resolution 256 produces geometrically correct meshes from the encoder alone, while resolution 8 starts with heavily degraded geometry that only partially recovers through training.
Latent recon loss is necessary but not sufficient: Both experiments converge the latent reconstruction loss (A: 2100→0.001, B: 1120→0.70) but this doesn't translate to proportional chamfer improvement. Chamfer is stable in both experiments after initial convergence. Direct mesh supervision (chamfer loss backpropagated through the decoder) is needed next.

5. Analysis

VALIDATED Resolution 256 produces geometrically meaningful meshes

Resolution 8 collapses hand/object to ~100-200 point blobs with pre-opt chamfer 0.08. Resolution 256 produces dense meshes with pre-opt chamfer 0.005 (15x better) that visually match GT shapes from initialization.

VALIDATED Trainable decoder path works end-to-end

The decoder runs in training mode with fp32 precision and explicit_gemm backend for 200 steps without errors. Gradients flow through decoder conv weights. Three Triton bugs resolved: signedness mismatch in tl.cdiv, dtype mismatch in tl.dot, uint32 torch.flip.

OBSERVATION Object chamfer does not improve with training

Both experiments show flat or slightly worse object chamfer after training. The latent recon loss optimizes token bridge projections but does not directly target mesh surface distance. Direct mesh supervision (chamfer loss backpropagated through the decoder) is needed for the next phase.
