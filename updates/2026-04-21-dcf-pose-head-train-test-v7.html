TL;DR

Generalisation gap is the headline. Every leg is 5–8× worse on the unseen-subject test sequence than on the seen-subject train sequence. B.00: 17 mm → 104 mm obj L2. C.00: 13 mm → 83 mm. The model is heavily overfitting to subject-01. Every downstream decision (pose head, balanced weights, Option B'') moves obj L2 by ~20 mm at most on the test set — noise compared to the 80+ mm overfit gap.
On the test set, C.00 is the best on object L2 (83.4 mm), D.2 best on hand L2 (72.8 mm). Rankings shift slightly from train but the overall story is: pose head helps obj, hurts hand; Option B'' (D.2) is the most balanced.
B.1 catastrophe persists on test. 719 mm obj L2 — same failure mode (Procrustes zeros global-translation gradient) generalises to unseen subjects.

Follow-ups

  • Address overfitting first. Any further architectural ablation is premature until generalisation improves. Candidates: train on multiple subjects (not just subject-01), data augmentation, regularisation on the DCF branch specifically.
  • Option C (residual reparametrisation) is still worth trying. None of D.0–D.2 closed the shape-drift gap to B.1/Procrustes. Only a structural fix (pred_traj = s·R·canonical + t + delta) can drive shape drift to ~0 without the position catastrophe.
  • Pose-rigid overfits more than trajectory on test. See D.0/D.1 pose-rigid L2 (154, 142) vs trajectory (115, 103). Pose head's 7-DoF output has higher bias on seen data than the 768-DoF trajectory, and the bias doesn't transfer.
  • Run full s1_test suite. This page uses one test sequence; the official DexYCB s1_test has ~200 sequences across subjects 7 and 8. A proper test eval should average over them.
