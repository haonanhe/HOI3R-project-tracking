6. Analysis

VALIDATED res=256 single-frame overfit with existing pipeline

The existing dexycb_trellis_overfit.py runs cleanly with --grid-size 256 for 500 steps. Latent reconstruction loss converges to 0.10 (99.99% reduction). The token bridge successfully learns to preserve SLat through the STream3R attention blocks.

VALIDATED Visual quality matches session 1 results

Post-training hand and object meshes show recognizable shapes aligned with GT, consistent with the session 1 overfit results at res=256. The visualization pipeline (denormalization, world-space scatter, overlay) works correctly.

OBSERVATION Chamfer plateau — latent recon loss doesn't drive surface improvement

Chamfer distance remains flat throughout training (~0.006 hand, ~0.009 object). The latent recon loss ensures the token bridge faithfully reconstructs encoder latents, but this doesn't directly translate to improved mesh surface quality. The encoder's reconstruction fidelity is the ceiling for this loss alone.

Next step: Add direct mesh supervision (chamfer loss or render-based loss backpropagated through the trainable decoder) to push chamfer below the encoder baseline.
