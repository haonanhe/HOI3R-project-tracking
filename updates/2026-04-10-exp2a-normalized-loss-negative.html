Analysis

FAILURE Loss normalization is counterproductive for converging losses

loss / loss.detach() maintains unit gradient magnitude regardless of loss value. When the latent loss converges toward zero, this amplifies its gradient, which:

  • Prevents the model from settling (latent loss keeps perturbing features)
  • Increases mesh loss (0.009 → 0.011) instead of letting it decrease
  • Eventually causes GPU OOM from large gradient magnitudes

CONCLUSION λ=0.001 remains the best approach

The reweighted approach (λ=0.001) naturally transitions from "latent-guided early" to "mesh-loss-driven late" as the latent converges. This two-phase behavior is a feature, not a bug. Final best: CD=0.043, λ=0.001, 1000 steps.
