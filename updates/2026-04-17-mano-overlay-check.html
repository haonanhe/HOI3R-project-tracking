Goal: The numeric MANO validation gate passed for HO3D and DexYCB, but a numeric pass only proves NaN-freeness, joint count, and value ranges. This page projects the loader's GT (hand vertices, hand 21-joint skeleton, object vertices) onto the source RGB to verify geometric alignment.

v2 update — bone topology rebuilt against the original_mano_reference bundle. The v1 rendering went through an intermediate MANO_TO_OPENPOSE permutation whose tip-vertex convention disagreed with what the smplx-MANO loader actually emits, leaving the thumb/pinky wiring visibly off on DexYCB. v2 drops the permutation and draws bones directly on the smplx-MANO 21-joint layout with tips 16..20 = [thb, idx, mid, rng, pky] (verts 744, 320, 443, 554, 671), matching the fingertip convention the reference adapter uses in manopth. Empirical check: tip→nearest-DIP distance on a flat hand; log at /tmp/verify_mano_tips.log on the debug container.

Legend & method

Hand vertices (778 verts) — semi-transparent Hand joints (21) + skeleton bones Object vertices — subsampled to 5k for legibility

Projection: (u, v) = (K @ (X / 1000) / Z)[:2] with X in mm camera-space, K in pixel units (per-frame, shape (T, 3, 3)). Points with Z ≤ 0 or off-image are filtered.

Datasets share the per-frame intrinsics returned by the GT loader (HO3D: 30 frames, DexYCB: 74 frames per chosen test sequence).

Skeleton bones (v2, no remap): five chains rooted at the wrist, using the smplx-MANO 21-joint layout directly.

thumb  : (0,13)(13,14)(14,15)(15,16)   # 16 = thb_tip   vert 744
