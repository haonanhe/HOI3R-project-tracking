Why B.1 failed — architectural insight

The procrustes_aligned_3d_loss in stream3r/loss/dcf_losses.py computes the closed-form rigid fit (R*, t*) = argmin ‖R·pred + t − gt‖² then returns L1(R*·pred + t*, gt). By construction, this loss is invariant to any (R, t) applied to pred — the Kabsch step absorbs it. So the head's training signal said "produce the right shape, but I don't care where you put it."

The head obliged: at inference it emits the correct shape (obj chamfer/L2 ratio is not catastrophic — 1.83 — so shape structure is recognizable), but translates the entire point cluster by ~780 mm from the true GT position. Every frame is consistently displaced, which is why obj L2 max (800) is only slightly above obj L2 mean (779) — the drift is stable across frames, not noisy.

Correct design for follow-on: use procrustes_aligned as a shape regularizer alongside the normal world-frame L1, not as a replacement. E.g., L = L1_world + λ · L_procrustes with small λ. The L1 term supplies pose gradient; the Procrustes term emphasizes shape fidelity.
