CONVERGES Branch: feat/dcf Commit: 5288853 ACP job: om-mpgfphqc

Follow-up to the 5-step A2 wiring smoke, which was too short to judge whether the newly-wired real DexYCB depth + camera scene loss (replacing the earlier distillation teacher) actually converges alongside hand/object trajectory losses. This run trains the same 5-frame sequence for 200 steps at the full scene-loss weight (--loss-weight-scene 1.0) and records training + eval curves every 5 / 20 steps.

TL;DR: All losses converge monotonically on 5 frames. Hand L2 drops from 257.2 mm to 12.5 mm (≈21× reduction); object L2 drops from 595.6 mm to 22.8 mm (≈26×). The "scene loss does not converge" concern was a false alarm from a 5-step smoke window — at 200 steps, scene loss decreases in lockstep with the trajectory losses and even becomes slightly negative due to the -α·log(conf) term in the confidence-weighted depth loss.

Interpretation — does scene loss converge?

Yes, under real DexYCB GT. On this 5-frame grasp snippet, the three scene-loss components (loss_conf, loss_grad, loss_camera) fall together with the DCF trajectory losses. The earlier conclusion that "scene loss does not converge" was drawn from the A2 smoke, which stopped at step 5 before any of the losses had a chance to settle.
Caveats: (1) This is overfit on 5 consecutive frames — the real test is whether scene loss still converges on the full 72-frame sequence and whether the depth / camera head tuning generalises. (2) With a single static camera, the camera-loss signal is degenerate (GT cam2world identical across frames); what converges here is effectively only the depth branch plus a trivial pose target. (3) Scene loss going negative is a red herring introduced by the conf reparameterisation — track the loss_grad and regression MSE components separately if we want a monotone diagnostic in future.

Follow-ups ranked

  1. Rerun with render-video (om-p4da8v7x) to attach camera-view overlays here; the current predictions.pt was saved without world_points_per_frame.
  2. Scale up to full 72-frame sequence at 200-500 steps to see if the same convergence holds without overfit.
  3. Decompose the scene-loss scalar in the training logs: log loss_conf, loss_grad, loss_camera separately instead of only their sum, so the "goes negative" artifact stops confusing convergence reads.
