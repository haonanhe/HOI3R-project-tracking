The fixes #1 and #4 don't just move the scalar — they turn on two separate supervision channels that the v1 scalar silently collapsed into noise. Watching them individually tells us what's fitting and what's gaming.

Spotting the −α·log(conf) entropy-gaming hazard flagged in the audit: the conf terms (loss_pts3d_conf, loss_depth_conf) can go negative when the optimiser lowers the confidence scalar without actually improving the regression. Compare the _conf curves with the _grad curves — grad only moves when the regression improves. If conf drops while grad plateaus, that's entropy gaming (especially dangerous when heads are unfrozen). On this run both _conf and _grad for depth drop together, so depth is genuinely improving. loss_camera also drops cleanly in v2-B (unfrozen heads can adapt FoV to DexYCB's stretched intrinsics) which was impossible in v1's single-view setting.

Interpretation & Phase-3 implication

What the +point_loss + multi-view + PP-crop actually did:
  1. Gave camera_loss a real target. In v1 the single-view setup made relative pose = identity, so camera_loss sat near zero contributing nothing. In v2 with 4 views per step, the predicted relative poses have non-trivial targets; both runs show non-zero loss_camera that drops over training.
  2. Gave the point_head direct world-space supervision. Previously only the depth_head received gradient from scene loss. Now point_head (32.7 M params) sees GT world pointmaps. In A (frozen) this still only shapes the backbone through its frozen Jacobian; in B (unfrozen) point_head can actively adapt, which is likely why B's L2 improved 3× vs v1.
  3. Removed the 65535mm outliers from the normalization denominator. Before fix #2, the median-based depth normalization was getting ~1% of pixels at 65 m which dragged the scale factor up. Now the mean/median depth scale is a clean 1.05 m on this scene.
  4. Bounded the data-prep anisotropy. PP-centered crop + Lanczos makes the intrinsic rescale cleaner (fx/fy ratio drops from 1.33 to 1.34 — still non-isotropic on DexYCB because the PP-crop itself is 618×462, but the image content is no longer stretched).
Does this flip the freezing policy? On this 5-frame smoke: no, frozen still wins (22.9 vs 39.9 mm hand). But the gap shrank from 6× to 1.7×, and the reason unfrozen was losing in v1 (scene heads drifting without purpose) has been fixed. Plan update:
  • Keep smoke-stage freezing policy until Phase-3 full-dataset rerun.
  • Phase 3 is where unfrozen should win — with 72-frame sequences and proper supervision, the scene heads will have enough signal that their extra 281 M capacity becomes adaptation, not overfit.
  • Expect the loss_pts3d_conf curve to be the canary: if it drops faster than loss_pts3d_grad, that's entropy gaming — pull the scene heads back or add an EMA regulariser.
