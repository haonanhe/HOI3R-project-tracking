Four videos

Video 4 — the "camera matches RGB input" trick

The DexYCB raw RGB input was recorded by the camera whose pose, in the 4RC world frame, is extr_c2w ≈ I at frame 0 (4RC sets its canonical-world origin at the first-frame camera center). For matplotlib's ax.view_init(elev, azim) to place the viewer at that same location, we need to:

  1. Rotate all world-space points by a fixed basis R so the DexYCB "scene up" (−Y_world) becomes matplotlib's +Z_mpl. The basis is R = [[1,0,0],[0,0,1],[0,−1,0]] (i.e., Y ↔ −Z).
  2. Compute the scene centroid in world coords (scene[0..T−1]["pts"] mean on the high-confidence mask).
  3. Call camera_pose_to_view_init(extr_c2w, centroid_world, world_basis=R), which takes the camera-in-world vector C − centroid, applies R once, and reads off elev = asin(v_z/|v|), azim = atan2(v_y, v_x).
  4. Sweep the azimuth by +total_rot × (t/(T−1)) across the clip. Default total_rot = 360°.

Open follow-ups

  • After the DexYCB handedness fix lands in a new Stage 1 overfit, re-run this pipeline on the new predictions.pt and publish a "post-handedness-fix" tracker entry with the same 4 videos.
  • Video 4's "camera matches RGB" is validated on a nearly-identity extr_c2w. For non-canonical 4RC clips (not yet used in HOI3R), the same camera_pose_to_view_init path applies but the matplotlib framing will need per-clip ax.set_box_aspect tuning.
  • Scene-track rendering in video 1 currently takes the track-query frame (default t=15) pixels. For clips where the query is mid-sequence, the trails include prediction looking both forward and backward from t=15 — an asymmetry that matches 4RC viser behaviour but may read as jittery on very-long clips.
