Key discoveries

FAIL Procrustes-aligned loss alone is catastrophic. B.1 and C.02 have obj L2 of 779 mm / 61 mm.

Kabsch removes optimal (R*, t*) before computing L1, so global translation gradient is zeroed. Training converges to "right shape, wrong place." Shape drift is near-perfect (0.11 mm) but world-frame position drifts hundreds of millimetres. Never use Procrustes as sole object loss.

FINDING Training loss ≠ eval metric when object_loss_mode differs.

Initial v1 publish used training-step loss_obj averaged over last 100 steps and claimed B.1 was best. That was Procrustes residual, not world-frame L2. Real eval on held-out seq showed B.1 = 779 mm. Publish pages must use infer_per_view_overfit.py held-out eval, not training history. See feedback_training_loss_vs_eval.md.

FAIL Subject-01 overfit dominates architectural differences.

On unseen subject-08 (s1_test): B.00 obj L2 = 104 mm (vs 17 on train). All C.x/D.x tuning lives in a 30 mm band on test, swamped by the 90 mm overfit gap. Any further arch ablation on subject-01-only training is contaminated. Priority invert: expand training manifest first.

FINDING Pose head helps obj L2 on train, costs hand L2 (shared aggregator capacity).

C.00 obj L2 13.05 vs B.00's 17.03 (23% better) — but hand 23.5 vs 14.0 (+68%). The pose head's 6 new loss terms on the shared decoder steal capacity from hand. D.1 (balanced hand_weight=3.7) recovers hand but regresses obj. Tradeoff is fundamental unless pose head gets dedicated feature path (Option C, not tried).

FINDING Option B'' (D.2) is the simplest winning configuration.

B.00 + GT-pose-relative inter-frame consistency (--shape-consistency-weight 1.0), no pose head. Test hand L2 72.85 (best; 5 mm better than B.00), test obj L2 101.62 (matches B.00). Shape drift 2.87 (best among non-Procrustes on test). No architectural complexity — just one extra loss.

FINDING Pose-head rigid is WORSE than trajectory on test (154 vs 115 mm for D.0).

7-DoF pose head has higher inductive bias than 768-DoF trajectory head. On seen data this means the pose head generalises less. Consumer must not always prefer pose-head-rigid over trajectory — depends on whether Procrustes-mode training is active (C.02 → yes, else → no).

FINDING Soft shape-regularisation bottoms out at 0.9–1.5 mm drift. Only Procrustes (or Option C structural) can go lower.

D.0's w_consist=2.0: 1.51 mm drift vs C.00's 1.41. D.2's Option B'': 1.45. None approach B.1's 0.11 mm. If shape-perfect rigidity is required, structural enforcement is the next step (pred_traj = s·R·canonical + t + delta, Option C from the spec).

Caveats

  • All eval numbers are from one sequence per split. Proper s1_test averaging pending.
  • Training data is subject-01 only. Every architectural finding assumes this confounded baseline.
  • Pose-head rotation accuracy is capped at ~15° by K=2048 codebook Voronoi cells; hasn't been raised.
  • Shape drift metric is inter-frame Procrustes residual. Alternative (per-frame rigid-fit residual) not computed.
  • infer_per_view_overfit.py auto-detection relies on object_pose_cls_token key; future pose-head variants may need explicit flag.
  • v1–v2 of the publish page were wrong (training loss confused with eval metric). v3–v7 are corrected; v1/v2 overwritten, not deleted.
